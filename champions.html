History of the Champions League

The UEFA Champions League, as we know today was rebranded in 1992. Prior to that the competition was originally named the Coupe des Clubs Champions Européens or more commomnly the European Cup, first established in 1955. It was intiailly a straight knockout tournament open only to the champions of Europe's domestic leagues, with the winner reckoned as the as the European club champions. In 1991 a round-robin group stage, allowing multiple entrants form certain countries since the 1997-98 season. It has since expanded further, while most of Europe's national leagues can still only enter their champion, the strongest leagues,Premier League, La Liga, Bundesliga, and Serie A get up to Four places in the competition each.
